# Uncapped PC Social Campaign Playbook
## Campaign: Build First. Review Before Buying.

**Audience:** Head of Marketing, Creative Director, Head of Tech, Social Media Manager, Sales/DM Team  
**Campaign Length:** 30 days initial launch, extendable to 90 days  
**Primary Objective:** Make customers use Uncapped PC's website as a guided PC build and quote tool, not just a product catalog.  
**Core Conversion:** Customer opens a campaign link, edits or reviews a build, generates a quote image or quote link, then requests approval/review from Uncapped PC.

---

## 1. Campaign Executive Summary

Uncapped PC should launch a campaign around one clear idea:

> **Build your PC first. Let us review it before you buy.**

The website should not be positioned as a standard e-commerce checkout flow yet. The current strength is a guided planning and quotation experience.

The customer can:
- Open a ready-made promo or bundle link.
- View prefilled or highlighted PC parts.
- Edit parts based on budget and preference.
- See bundle and promo discounts.
- Unlock add-on savings where eligible.
- Generate a clean quote image.
- Save builds to their account.
- Request approval or staff review before payment.

This supports Uncapped PC's broader brand positioning:

> **Honest PC upgrade guidance, backed by clear tools and structured support.**

This campaign should make the website feel like a safer way to buy PC parts because the buyer is not forced to understand everything alone.

---

## 2. The Strategic Problem

### Current customer problem
Many customers want to build or upgrade a PC, but they are unsure about:
- Which CPU and motherboard combination fits their budget.
- Whether the parts they chose are compatible.
- Whether they are overspending.
- Whether the full build price is still within budget.
- Whether the build can be reviewed before committing.
- How to send the build to family, friends, or someone more technical for approval.

### Business problem
Uncapped PC still relies heavily on manual conversation to explain builds, bundles, and options. This limits scale.

### Campaign solution
Move customers from unstructured chat into a structured website journey:

**Social post → tracked promo or bundle link → PC Builder → quote image / saved build → request review → staff approval → sale**

This allows marketing, sales, and tech to work around one shared system instead of each post starting from scratch.

## 3. Campaign Positioning

### Public-facing positioning
**Build your PC first. Review before buying.**

### Supporting lines
- See your full PC build price before messaging.
- Open a ready-made build and adjust the parts.
- Generate a quote image you can save or send.
- Request Uncapped PC review before payment.
- Save your build and come back later.
- Eligible bundles can unlock extra add-on discounts.

### Internal positioning
Uncapped PC is not just selling parts. It is creating a safer decision process for customers.

### What this campaign must NOT imply
Do not claim:
- Instant checkout is complete.
- Compatibility is automatically guaranteed.
- Stock and pricing are always final without staff review.
- Payment can proceed without approval if that flow is not finalized.
- FPS Finder is live unless confirmed.
- Quote price is final forever.

Approved language:
- “Request review.”
- “Review before payment.”
- “Quote-ready build.”
- “Staff will confirm availability, discounts, and final pricing.”
- “Promo applies when required parts remain selected.”
- “Actual availability and pricing are reviewed before final confirmation.”

## 5. Core Customer Journey

### Journey A. Promo Build Journey
Best for featured bundles and campaign-specific posts.

1. Customer sees a Facebook Page post, Marketplace post, Discord announcement, or website banner.
2. Customer clicks a tracked promo link.
3. PC Builder opens with a promo build or required parts prefilled/highlighted.
4. Customer adjusts optional parts if needed.
5. Customer sees subtotal, savings, and quote details.
6. Customer generates a quote image or share link.
7. Customer requests approval.
8. Staff reviews availability, discounts, and final pricing.
9. Sale continues through the approved payment/order flow.

### Journey B. Bundle Masterlist Journey
Best for customers who are comparing CPU + motherboard options.

1. Customer sees a bundle post.
2. Customer opens the bundle masterlist.
3. Customer chooses a CPU + motherboard pair.
4. Customer sends the bundle into PC Builder.
5. Customer adds RAM, cooler, GPU, SSD, PSU, case, and other parts.
6. Customer generates a quote image.
7. Customer requests review.

### Journey C. Save Your Build Journey
Best for customers not ready to buy today.

1. Customer opens PC Builder.
2. Customer creates or edits a build.
3. Customer logs in.
4. Customer saves the build to My Builds.
5. Customer returns later to reopen, rename, share, archive, or screenshot the build.
6. Staff can review when customer is ready.

## 9. Creative Direction

### Visual mood
The campaign should look:
- Clean.
- Technical.
- Organized.
- Trustworthy.
- Easy to read on mobile.

It should not look:
- Like a loud price flyer.
- Like a generic gaming poster.
- Like a random marketplace listing.
- Like a completed checkout promise.

### Visual system
Use the Uncapped PC visual identity:
- Base black: `#0B0D10`
- Soft black: `#151A21`
- White: `#F5F7FA`
- Accent blue: `#2D7CFF`

### Design rules
- One main message per graphic.
- Use screenshots as proof, not decoration.
- Use large readable headlines.
- Use one accent color only.
- Use the Uncapped icon as a small signature, preferably bottom right.
- Avoid cluttered part lists unless the asset is specifically a quote image.

### Recommended motifs
- Blue highlight bar for important savings or CTA.
- Small context panels for build specs.
- Step cards for process explanations.
- Quote image frame as the campaign's recurring visual anchor.
- PC Builder screenshot inside a device or browser frame.

## 11. Head of Marketing Brief

### Marketing objective
Drive qualified traffic to PC Builder and bundle pages, then convert that traffic into quote requests.

### Primary KPIs
Track weekly:
- PC Builder link clicks.
- Promo link opens by source.
- Quote image generations.
- Quote/request approval submissions.
- Saved builds.
- Messenger conversations from campaign CTAs.
- Sales from quote codes where possible.

### Secondary KPIs
- Post saves.
- Shares.
- Comments asking for specs/recommendation.
- Direct brand searches.
- Repeat usage from saved builds.

### Channel priority
Primary:
1. Facebook Page
2. Facebook Marketplace

Secondary:
3. Discord
4. Website banners/announcement bars

Optional:
5. TikTok/Reels for demo videos

### Messaging priority
Rank campaign messages in this order:
1. Build first, review before buying.
2. See bundle pricing clearly.
3. Open ready-made promo builds.
4. Generate quote images.
5. Save your build.
6. Unlock cooler add-on discounts.

### Offer priority
Use these in order:
1. CPU + motherboard bundles.
2. Ready-made promo builds.
3. Cooler add-on discounts.
4. My Builds account feature.

### Link discipline
Every campaign post must use a tracked link.

Format:
```text
https://www.uncappedpc.com/pages/pc-builder-2?promo=PROMO_HANDLE&src=CHANNEL&campaign=CAMPAIGN_NAME&content=CREATIVE_NAME
```

Bundle masterlist format:
```text
https://www.uncappedpc.com/pages/bundle-masterlist?src=CHANNEL&campaign=CAMPAIGN_NAME&content=CREATIVE_NAME
```

Naming rules:
- `src`: channel source.
- `campaign`: month + offer.
- `content`: creative or post version.

Examples:
```text
https://www.uncappedpc.com/pages/pc-builder-2?promo=9800x3d-bundle&src=fb_page&campaign=may_9800x3d&content=carousel_v1

https://www.uncappedpc.com/pages/pc-builder-2?promo=7500f-ayw-gaming-wifi-bundle&src=fb_marketplace&campaign=may_7500f&content=main_listing

https://www.uncappedpc.com/pages/bundle-masterlist?src=discord&campaign=may_bundle_masterlist&content=announcement
```

### Campaign reporting
Create a weekly report with:
- Top 5 links by opens.
- Top 5 posts by engagement.
- Quote requests by campaign.
- Saved builds count.
- Sales or pending transactions from quote codes.
- Notes from DMs: most common questions, confusion points, objections.

Ensure the website features used in campaign are reliable, trackable, and clearly labeled.

- Promo links load correct required parts.
- Promo labels are visible.
- Promo discount appears only when required parts remain selected.
- Bundle discount appears clearly.
- Add-on discount appears clearly.
- Quote image generation works on desktop and mobile.

#### Bundle Pages
- Bundle options show accurate prices.
- Sorting by lowest price works.
- “Open in PC Builder” button works.
- Screenshot generation works if used for social assets.

#### My Builds
- Login requirement is clear.
- Save build works.
- Rename works.
- Open build works.
- Screenshot/share works.
- Archive/delete behavior is clear.

#### Tracking
- `src`, `campaign`, and `content` are captured.
- Promo handle is captured.
- Quote code is captured where available.
- Device/browser/page URL tracking is working.
- Link opens are logged to the correct sheet/database.

#### Labels and disclaimers
Make sure the site language does not overpromise.

Recommended labels:
- “Request review”
- “Generate quote image”
- “Save build”
- “Open in PC Builder”
- “Promo applies when required parts remain selected”
- “Staff will review availability, discounts, and final pricing before payment”

Avoid:
- “Instant checkout”
- “Guaranteed compatibility”
- “Final price guaranteed”
- “Live stock guaranteed”

### Tech support during launch
For the first 7 days:
- Check link tracking daily.
- Monitor promo link errors.
- Confirm quote image generation works across devices.
- Collect bug reports from sales/marketing.
- Create a changelog for campaign-critical fixes.

## 20. Risk Controls

### Risk 1. Customers think checkout is final
Control:
- Use “request review” and “review before payment.”
- Add staff review note in captions and website copy.

### Risk 2. Customers think compatibility is guaranteed
Control:
- Say PC Builder organizes the build. Staff reviews before payment.
- Do not say automatic compatibility guarantee.

### Risk 3. Promo breaks when parts change
Control:
- Use simple caption note:
  > Promo applies when required parts remain selected.

### Risk 4. Discount system feels confusing
Control:
Marketing should simplify:
- Bundle discount = CPU + motherboard savings.
- Promo discount = campaign offer.
- Add-on discount = extra savings after qualifying.

### Risk 5. Tracking becomes useless
Control:
- Every campaign post must use tracked links.
- No untagged links in campaign assets.

### Risk 6. Stock/pricing changes
Control:
- Use “subject to review before final confirmation.”
- Avoid “final price guaranteed.”
