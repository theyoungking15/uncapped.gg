# Uncapped PC Bundle-First Social Campaign Playbook

**Campaign working name:** Start With The Bundle  
**Prepared for:** Brand Analyst, Head of Marketing, Creative Director, Sales/Admin Team  
**Primary campaign focus:** CPU + motherboard bundles  
**Secondary support system:** Bundle Masterlist, PC Builder, Quote Code, Share Build, Save Build, Add-on Cooler Discount  
**Main sales goal:** Turn bundle interest into qualified DMs, quote codes, reviewed quotes, and closed sales  
**Primary platforms:** Facebook Page, Facebook Marketplace, Facebook Groups  
**Secondary platforms:** Instagram Reels, Instagram Carousels, YouTube Shorts  
**Optional platform:** TikTok reposts only after content is proven on Facebook or Instagram

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The campaign should not lead with the PC Builder.

The campaign should lead with the bundle.

The PC Builder is the tool that helps the customer complete the purchase journey. The bundle is the reason they care.

### Main customer-facing idea

**Start with the right CPU + motherboard bundle. Then complete the rest of your build with Uncapped PC.**

### Strongest campaign message

**Pick your bundle first. We help you complete the rest.**

### Why this is stronger than leading with PC Builder

Most buyers do not wake up wanting to use a PC Builder.

They want:

- Better FPS.
- A reliable upgrade.
- A CPU + motherboard combo that makes sense.
- Clear pricing.
- Someone to review the build before they pay.
- Less risk of buying the wrong parts.

The PC Builder supports that journey, but the offer must be the hook.

## 2. Campaign Positioning

### One-line positioning

**Uncapped PC helps buyers start with a verified CPU + motherboard bundle, then complete the build through a reviewed quote process.**

### Public-facing campaign line options

Preferred:

**Start with the bundle. Complete the build with us.**

Other options:

- Pick your bundle. Build smarter.
- AM5 bundles made easier.
- Your AM5 build starts here.
- Bundle first. Review before payment.
- Choose the combo. We help with the rest.

### Message hierarchy

1. **Bundle first**  
   CPU + motherboard bundles are the main offer.

2. **Guidance second**  
   We help customers choose based on goal, budget, and tradeoffs.

3. **PC Builder third**  
   The PC Builder helps them complete, save, share, screenshot, and request a quote.

4. **Review before payment**  
   Final pricing, availability, discounts, and compatibility are reviewed before payment.

5. **Support after purchase**  
   Uncapped PC is not just selling parts. Support is part of the value.

## 3. Campaign Objectives

### Main business objective

Sell more CPU + motherboard bundles by making the buying process easier, clearer, and safer.

### Secondary objectives

1. Drive traffic to the Bundle Masterlist.
2. Drive qualified users into the PC Builder.
3. Increase DMs with specific bundle interest.
4. Increase quote codes sent by customers.
5. Increase full build quote requests.
6. Increase add-on cooler attach rate.
7. Create repeatable content templates for Facebook, Instagram, and YouTube Shorts.
8. Build trust around review, support, and compatibility checking.

### Non-objectives

Do not position the website as full self-checkout if checkout is not enabled.

Do not imply:

- Instant checkout.
- Final price guarantee without review.
- Guaranteed compatibility without verification.
- Live stock guarantee.
- Cheapest bundle in the market.
- Best build for everyone.

Use:

**Request quote. Reviewed quote. Final pricing and availability will be confirmed.**

## 4. Target Audience

## Primary Audience: Performance Upgraders

People upgrading an existing PC who want better FPS, smoother performance, or a more modern platform without wasting money.

### Common buyer fears

- Buying the wrong motherboard.
- BIOS compatibility issues.
- Overpaying for an upgrade that does not change results.
- DDR5 RAM pricing being too expensive.
- Warranty stress.
- DOA items.
- Not knowing whether the full build makes sense.

### Message to them

**Start with a CPU + motherboard bundle that fits your goal. Then send the full build for review before payment.**

---

## Secondary Audience: Validators and PC Friends

Friends, group admins, tech coworkers, small builders, and experienced PC users who influence buying decisions.

### Message to them

**Your friend can send you the build link. You can check it, edit it, and send it back before they request a quote.**

This is important because the share feature gives knowledgeable people a reason to interact with the Uncapped PC website.

## 5. Core Offer Architecture

The campaign should connect all website features into one buying journey.

| Offer or Feature | Role in Campaign | Customer-Facing Message |
|---|---|---|
| CPU + Motherboard Bundles | Main offer | Start your build with a CPU + motherboard combo. |
| Bundle Masterlist | Comparison tool | Compare available bundle options before deciding. |
| Hero Bundle | Sales focus | Build around a specific high-interest CPU bundle. |
| PC Builder | Completion tool | Add the rest of your parts and request a reviewed quote. |
| Share Build | Validation tool | Let a friend check or edit your build before buying. |
| Quote Code | Conversion action | Send us your quote code so we can review the build. |
| Add-on Cooler Discount | Upsell | Selected bundles may unlock cooler discounts. |
| Review Before Payment | Trust proof | We check availability, discounts, compatibility, and final pricing. |

### Key principle

Do not market these as separate features.

Market them as one guided buying process:

**Choose bundle -> Complete build -> Share if needed -> Send quote code -> Review before payment.**

## 7. 30-Day Campaign Strategy

## Campaign theme

**Start With The Bundle**

### Month 1 focus

Make customers understand one thing:

**Uncapped PC bundles are the starting point for a reviewed PC build.**

The PC Builder should appear in the campaign, but only as the tool that completes the bundle journey.

### Content mix

| Content Type | Percentage | Reason |
|---|---:|---|
| Bundle offer posts | 35% | Direct sales and product awareness. |
| Bundle decision guidance | 25% | Builds trust and reduces wrong-parts anxiety. |
| PC Builder completion posts | 15% | Teaches the tool without making it the headline. |
| Add-on cooler discount posts | 10% | Increases average order value. |
| Review and trust posts | 10% | Reduces friction and builds safety. |
| Customer proof or light content | 5% | Adds credibility and familiarity. |

### Default weekly output

Default to **5 posts per week**.

Recommended weekly split:

- 2 bundle offer or bundle comparison posts.
- 1 bundle decision guidance post.
- 1 PC Builder or quote code education post.
- 1 trust, review, testimonial, or add-on post.

This is enough to create consistency without overloading production.

## 13. Measurement Plan

## Weekly metrics

| Metric | Why It Matters | Decision to Make |
|---|---|---|
| Bundle Masterlist clicks | Shows demand for bundle comparison | Improve poster and table if clicks are low. |
| Bundle-specific DMs | Shows direct buying interest | Push the bundle with highest qualified DMs. |
| Quote codes received | Strongest intent signal before sale | Repeat the creative that generated quote codes. |
| PC Builder sessions | Shows website engagement | Improve website CTA if sessions are low. |
| Add-on cooler attach rate | Measures upsell effectiveness | Change cooler creative or discount framing if low. |
| Qualified DMs | Measures real sales potential | Create more decision guidance if DMs are low quality. |
| Closed sales | Final business metric | Shift campaign focus toward bundles that close. |

## Weekly review questions

1. Which post generated the most qualified DMs?
2. Which bundle got the most quote requests?
3. Which post drove users to the Bundle Masterlist?
4. Which creative caused confusion?
5. Which objections appeared repeatedly in comments or DMs?
6. Did any post make claims that need more proof?
7. Which bundle should be the next hero offer?

## 16. Creative Director Instructions

## Core creative direction

The creative must make the bundle the hero.

The PC Builder should appear as the support tool, not the headline.

### Correct hierarchy

1. CPU + motherboard bundle.
2. Price or savings if verified.
3. Main tradeoff or use case.
4. PC Builder support feature.
5. CTA to DM or send quote code.

### Wrong hierarchy

1. PC Builder feature.
2. Website buttons.
3. Tool explanation.
4. Bundle mentioned at the end.

That makes the post feel like software marketing, not a sales campaign.

## 18. Creative Rules

### Must do

- Put the bundle name in the headline.
- Keep one main idea per creative.
- Make price, savings, or CTA easy to read within 3 seconds.
- Use real screenshots when possible.
- Use actual product photos when possible.
- Use “Choose this if” and “Skip this if” for recommendation posts.
- Include review disclaimer when showing PC Builder, quote codes, or pricing.

### Avoid

- Too many icons.
- Too much website UI in one image.
- Showing all features at once.
- Hype language.
- Unverified FPS claims.
- Small unreadable price tables.
- Burying the CTA.

### Recommended look

- Clean tech retail style.
- High contrast text.
- Product cutouts or clean product photography.
- Rounded cards.
